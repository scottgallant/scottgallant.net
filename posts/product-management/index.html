The product management role is the most important role in a (product) company. If your product sucks, everything gets harder and the company is at risk of failing. If your product is the best, everything gets easier and it's like a force multiplier on success. An excellent product is the foundation of a successfull company and this all hinges on the product manager.

There is a lot of advice out there on this topic. I've summarized my favorite learnings about the product management role in this article.

What is Product Management?

My two favorite definitions:

Product management is the art, science, and practice of making successful products (or making existing products successful).[1]
- Shreyas Doshi - Former Stripe, Twitter, Google, Yahoo

Product management is making good decisions about what to build, when.[2]
- Glen Coates is the VP of Product Shopify

When is a Product Manager (PM) Successful?

When your product solves the problem better than everyone else so customers choose you.[3]
- Paraphrased from Marty Cagan - Founder of Sillicon Valley Product Group

Product Manager Success Metrics

These metrics should trend in a positive direction when the PM is doing a good job:
  • User adoption
  • Business impact (i.e. revenue)
  • Customer satisfaction

Product Manager Responsibilities

PMs make sure the most import user problems are solved by cross-functional product teams in a way that works for the business.

This requires expert-level knowledge in:

  • The product
  • The users
  • The data
  • The competitive landscape [4]


Product managers also need to deeply understand the different parts of the business - how the product is marketed, sold, compliance issues, security issues, etc.

As described in this video PMs are responsible for more than just prioritizing a backlog. They are creators that work with the product team throughout the entire process.

Product Team Responsibilities

Marty Cagan suggests that each person of the team is responsible for certain product "risks":
Product Risk Who's responsible?
Valuable - do users want it? Product Manager
Feasible - can we do it? Engineers
Usable - can users figure it out? Designer
Viable - does it make sense for the business? Product Manager

Attributes of a good PM

  • Immense user empathy
  • Deep care/obsession
  • Low ego
  • High Agency
  • Ownership Mindset
  • Influential communication

In my view, high agency individuals push through when faced with adverse conditions instead of blaming their circumstances.

Influencial communication is important because it can be hard to get a team focused on the right problem to solve. I love Shreya's tweet:

Conclusion

Ultimately, the foundation of a successful company lies in its product, and the product manager is the linchpin of that success. Setting that person up for success is essential for the future of the business.
